Climate Change Explained

Understanding its impact, causes, and what we can do.

What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term alterations in temperature, precipitation, wind patterns, and other aspects of the Earth's climate system. It’s largely driven by human activities, particularly the burning of fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial emissions.

Major Causes

  • Greenhouse gases like CO₂ and CH₄ trap heat in the atmosphere.
  • Deforestation reduces the Earth’s capacity to absorb carbon dioxide.
  • Industrial activity and urbanization lead to excessive emissions and pollution.

Impact on the Planet

  • Rising sea levels threatening coastal cities.
  • More frequent and intense wildfires and hurricanes.
  • Melting glaciers and disrupted ecosystems.
  • Declining biodiversity and agricultural productivity.

Interesting Statistics

  • Earth’s temperature has risen by 1.2°C since the pre-industrial era.
  • Arctic sea ice has declined by over 13% per decade since 1980.
  • 2023 was the hottest year ever recorded globally.

Solutions & Mitigations

  • Shifting to renewable energy sources (solar, wind, hydro).
  • Planting more trees and conserving forests.
  • Implementing carbon taxes and emissions regulations.
  • Improving public transportation and encouraging electric vehicles.

Global Effects & Regional Trends

Heatwaves in Europe

Europe has witnessed a 3-fold increase in heatwaves over the past two decades, impacting human health and energy systems.

Flooding Patterns in Asia

South Asian nations have experienced a 50% increase in urban flooding incidents since 2000, linked directly to climate variability.

Wildfire Risk in Australia

Australia’s bushfire season now starts earlier and ends later each year. 2019–2020 fires burned over 18 million hectares.

Additional Climate Sections

Coral Bleaching Trends

Rising ocean temperatures lead to coral expelling algae, causing bleaching and threatening marine ecosystems.

Permafrost Thawing

Melting permafrost releases methane, a potent greenhouse gas, accelerating climate warming in Arctic regions.

Shifting Climate Zones

As global temperatures rise, climate zones shift, disrupting agriculture and biodiversity globally.

Air Quality Degradation

Higher temperatures and emissions contribute to smog formation, reducing urban air quality and impacting health.

Climate Migration

Extreme weather events and sea-level rise displace communities, creating climate refugees across continents.

Ocean Acidification

CO₂ absorption lowers ocean pH levels, threatening shell-forming organisms and disrupting food chains.

Urban Heat Islands

Concrete and infrastructure trap heat, raising city temperatures and increasing energy use and health risks.

Water Scarcity

Changing rainfall patterns and glacier loss reduce freshwater availability for millions globally.

Renewable Energy Growth

Wind and solar power adoption is accelerating, replacing fossil fuels and cutting carbon emissions.

Global Emission Trends

Countries are adopting climate agreements like the Paris Accord to reduce national greenhouse gas emissions.
